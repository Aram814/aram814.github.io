CPR Ready

TL;DR: CPR Ready is a mobile-first UX project designed to help adults learn, practice, and retain CPR skills through a clear, structured, and interactive experience. The app includes modules for Learning, Practicing, Tracking Progress, and Emergency Mode for real-time guidance. While no live user testing was conducted, AI-based simulations informed early usability feedback. The focus was on delivering accuracy, clarity, and preparedness to ensure confidence in life-saving situations.

Overview Research & Insights Design Process The Solution Impact & Results

1. Project Overview

Goal: Create a mobile experience that makes learning and retaining CPR skills accessible, engaging, and effective.

Challenge: CPR is high-stakes and time-sensitive; users need confidence in their ability to perform correctly in an emergency, even months after training. Traditional methods (in-person courses, static guides) often fail to reinforce long-term knowledge retention.

Scope: Designed mobile-first in Figma. Focused on UI/UX flows for four key modules: Learn, Practice, Progress, and Emergency Mode. Created as a UX case study project, not a production-ready app.

2. Research & Insights

User Pain Points (assumed & AI-tested):

  • Forgetting CPR steps after initial training.
  • Lack of opportunities to practice outside of emergencies.
  • Overwhelming information in stressful moments.

Constraints:

  • Extreme importance of accurate, medically sound details.
  • Needed balance between simplicity (for stressed users) and depth (for practice/learning).

Testing Approach: No formal user testing. Relied on AI-based scenario testing to simulate interactions and evaluate usability.

3. Design Process

Ideation: Sketched wireframes for each mode (Learn, Practice, Progress, Emergency). Explored interaction flows that reduce friction during high-stress use cases.

Prototyping: Built in Figma with focus on clear hierarchy, bold visual cues, and simple navigation.

Key Decisions: Use of consistent icons and color coding across modes. Emergency Mode designed as a single-screen, step-by-step guide.

4. The Solution (Key Features)

The final deliverable was a live prototype of a CPR guidance app with the following features:

  • Learn Mode: Step-by-step lessons explaining CPR fundamentals. Simple visuals paired with concise text.
  • Practice Mode (standout feature): Interactive exercises simulating CPR steps. Gives users a chance to rehearse and build muscle memory.
  • Progress Mode: Tracks user learning milestones. Next steps: integrate goals, badges, and achievements to motivate retention.
  • Emergency Mode: High-contrast, distraction-free interface. Real-time guidance in short actionable steps.

5. Impact & Results

Outcome: Delivered a polished Figma prototype demonstrating a mobile-first CPR training experience. Showcased how digital learning tools can reinforce life-saving skills beyond traditional training.

What Worked Well:

  • Practice Mode created a unique “safe rehearsal” environment.
  • Emergency Mode interface reduced overwhelm with its simplicity.

Next Steps:

  • Explore gamification in Progress Mode (badges, streaks, achievements).
  • Conduct real user testing to validate usability in stress conditions.
  • Partner with CPR-certified organizations for medical content validation.
