QueerSafeSpace

TL;DR: QueerSafeSpace is a community-powered mobile app that helps LGBTQ+ individuals find safe spaces in public environments. The app combines user-submitted ratings, location tagging, and descriptive safety information to empower users to navigate spaces confidently.

Overview Challenge Research Ideation & Design Solution Impact

Project Overview

Project Type: UX Design / Mobile App

Role: Sole UX Designer & Researcher

Timeline: 10–12 weeks

Tools Used: Figma, FlutterFlow, Supabase, Tally Forms, Miro

The Challenge

Problem Statement: LGBTQ+ people often face uncertainty in public spaces, and reliable safety information is scattered or nonexistent. Traditional solutions (online forums, anecdotal posts) are inconsistent, anonymous, or outdated.

Why It Matters: Safety and inclusion are critical concerns for the LGBTQ+ community. Without a trusted resource, individuals risk unsafe experiences or are forced to limit their activities.

Goals:

  • Create a trustworthy, community-sourced safety map.
  • Ensure anonymity and privacy for contributors.
  • Make it mobile-friendly and accessible.
  • Allow filtering by location type and safety level.

Research & Discovery

Methods: User interviews (targeted LGBTQ+ community members), Surveys to gauge pain points with existing safety information, Competitive analysis of similar apps (e.g., Shane).

Key Findings: Users want quick, actionable information when visiting unfamiliar spaces. Safety levels should be clearly categorized (Friendly, Mixed, Unsafe, Unknown). Descriptive context (one-word or short comments) is valuable for nuanced experiences. Community trust and anonymity are essential for adoption.

Personas: “The Explorer” – Seeks new safe spaces for socializing. “The Protector” – Wants to contribute to community knowledge and warn others about unsafe areas.

Ideation & Design Process

I created user flows, wireframes, and prototypes to translate research findings into a tangible product. The design language focuses on a calm, inclusive aesthetic to resonate with the target audience.

  • Wireframes & Flows: Splash page → Log in/Sign up → Permissions → Main map. Map interface includes filters for categories (e.g., cafe, bar, entertainment). Submission form for new locations includes safety tag and descriptive text.
  • Design Considerations: Visual clarity for safety ratings using color codes (green, yellow, red, gray). Minimal friction for submitting a location while maintaining anonymity. Accessible typography, iconography, and color contrast.
  • Iteration: Early sketches were reviewed with community members. Feedback incorporated to simplify the submission flow and improve map filtering.

The Solution

The final product is a mobile app with a suite of features designed to meet player needs, including:

  • Features: Community-sourced map with safety ratings. Location filters and search functionality. “Find My Safe Space” and “Crisis Mode” quick-access buttons. Anonymous submissions with optional descriptions. Color-coded safety levels.
  • Prototype: Interactive Figma prototype available. Screenshots for splash, login/signup, main map, and submission flow.

Impact & Results

The app was well-received in user testing, with participants praising its focus on safety and community.

  • Community Feedback: Positive responses on clarity, usability, and trustworthiness.
  • Next Steps: Expand database of locations via community engagement. Add optional features like user badges or contribution tracking. Potential integration with safety alerts and events.
  • Metrics to Track: Number of active users and submissions. Map searches and filter usage. User retention over time.
