Send Me On My Way

TL;DR: Send Me On My Way is a calming, nature-inspired website created for Jennifer Thomas, a death doula in Ocala, FL. The challenge was to design a web presence that communicates comfort, clarity, and trust without being clinical. My solution was a GitHub-hosted site with an earthy sage palette, gentle typography, and a soon-to-be hand-drawn logo. It introduces Jennifer, her services, and resources while enabling future booking and contact via Supabase backend integration. The result is a professional, empathetic, and sustainable site foundation that grows alongside Jennifer’s practice.

Project Overview

Project Type: UX Design / Web Presence

Role: Sole UX Designer

Timeline: Iterative Build

Tools Used: Figma, GitHub Pages, Supabase

The Challenge

Problem Statement: Death doulas provide essential support during life’s most vulnerable moments, yet the role is often misunderstood. Jennifer needed a website that explained her services in a way that felt approachable and trustworthy. Many existing doula sites lean either overly clinical or overly abstract, leaving visitors confused or uncomfortable.

Why It Matters: The need for a web presence that could explain her services without being clinical.

Goals:

  • Create a space that feels grounded, compassionate, and spiritual — a reflection of Jennifer’s philosophy and style.
  • Communicate comfort, clarity, and trust without being clinical.
  • Allow for future booking and contact via a Supabase backend integration.
  • Host the site on GitHub Pages due to a small budget.

Research & Discovery

Methods: I worked closely with Jennifer to capture her voice and tone. She emphasized avoiding clinical aesthetics and wanted the design to reflect nature, spirituality, and comfort.

Key Findings: A competitive review identified gaps in existing doula sites (too medical vs. too poetic). Client input specified an earthy sage color palette, a hand-drawn line logo of two hands (pending), and gentle typography. The key insight was that visitors care most about who Jennifer is, what she offers, and how to contact her — clarity is more valuable than heavy symbolism.

Personas: Visitors care about who Jennifer is, what she offers, and how to contact her.

Ideation & Design Process

The site was structured with simplicity and scalability in mind. The information architecture was designed to be straightforward, and the visual design reflects a calming, spiritual aesthetic.

  • Information Architecture: The site was structured with pages for Home, About, Services, Blog, Contact, and Gallery (future).
  • Visual Design: A sage green primary color was used, paired with neutral tones. The typography uses Inter for the body and a warm serif for headings. The design incorporates soft rounded sections and ample white space for a calming rhythm.
  • Iteration: The site was built to be an iterative, with a foundation that allows for future expansion.

The Solution

The result is a professional, empathetic, and sustainable site foundation that grows alongside Jennifer’s practice. It is a responsive, GitHub-hosted site that establishes Jennifer’s digital presence while leaving room to expand as her practice grows.

  • GitHub static hosting: Keeps the site lightweight and affordable.
  • Supabase backend: Planned for handling contact and inquiries.
  • Blog subpage: Already active, providing a space for Jennifer’s reflections and resources.
  • Responsive design: Ensures accessibility across devices.

Impact & Results

The website was well-received by Jennifer, and it now serves as a professional, empathetic web presence that reflects her voice and values.
