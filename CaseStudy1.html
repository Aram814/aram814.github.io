Pixelated Scorecade

TL;DR: Pixelated Scorecade is a retro-inspired mobile app that reimagines the way arcade players track and share high scores. The challenge was to modernize an outdated process—paper scoreboards and fragmented notes—while keeping the nostalgia of classic gaming culture. My solution was a mobile app with a pixel-art interface that allows users to log scores, view leaderboards, and compete across locations. The result is a playful, user-friendly product that blends retro aesthetics with modern UX patterns.

Overview Challenge Research Ideation & Design Solution Impact

Project Overview

Project Type: UX Design Case Study

Role: Sole UX Designer & Researcher

Timeline: 8 weeks

Tools Used: Figma, React, Photoshop

The Challenge

Problem Statement: Arcade high score tracking is outdated, inconsistent, and inaccessible.

Why It Matters: Modern arcade players wanted a way to share achievements digitally while preserving arcade culture.

Goals: Build a digital system that feels authentic to retro gaming and fosters community competition.

Research & Discovery

Methods: Competitive analysis of score-tracking apps, 5 player interviews, and contextual arcade observation.

Key Findings: Players valued nostalgia, hated cluttered interfaces, and wanted social features.

Personas: “The Competitor” and “The Collector” emerged as primary user types.

Ideation & Design Process

I created user flows, wireframes, and prototypes to translate research findings into a tangible product. The design language focuses on a pixel-art aesthetic to resonate with the target audience.

  • Wireframes: Started with simple flows for score entry and leaderboard browsing.
  • Iterations: Tested 3 leaderboard layouts; users preferred the vertical scrolling list with badges.
  • Visual Style: Chose a pixelated UI with neon accents to merge retro and modern.
  • Prototyping: Interactive React prototype built for testing.

The Solution

The final product is a mobile app with a suite of features designed to meet player needs, including:

  • Final Design: A mobile app with streamlined score entry, player profiles, and cross-location leaderboards.
  • Add high scores instantly: Users can easily log scores with a photo verification system.
  • Retro badges + pixel art UI: Each user gets a customizable pixelated avatar and profile page to showcase their achievements.
  • Location-based leaderboards: Players can view top scores for their favorite games, both globally and at specific arcade locations.
  • Accessibility: High-contrast palette and large text for arcade lighting conditions.
  • Social Community: Friends can follow each other, like scores, and challenge one another to beat high scores.

Impact & Results

The app was well-received in user testing, with participants praising its unique aesthetic and ease of use. Key performance indicators (KPIs) showed promising initial results:

Reflection: This project demonstrated the value of combining deep user research with a cohesive, on-brand design. Future iterations could include a tournament-hosting feature and integration with more retro games.
